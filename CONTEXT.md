# Personal Blog

A personal site for sharing technical findings and write-ups, primarily read by the author and a small circle of curious peers. May later grow a curated CV-style surface that links out to selected posts.

## Language

**Coffee Coded**:
The site brand and public identity. Used in site title, navigation, meta tags, and anywhere a reader interacts with the site as a destination. _Not_ used in first-person prose — the author writes as "Mohsen."
_Avoid_: Mixing it into content where "I'm Mohsen" would be clearer.

**Post**:
A long-form technical write-up (typically 500–3000 words). The dominant content type.
_Avoid_: Article, entry, blog post (in code/folder names — just use `post`).

**TIL**:
A short note (typically 50–300 words) capturing a single thing learned — a flag, a gotcha, a snippet. Deferred: not built on day one, added once the post-publishing habit is established.
_Avoid_: Note, snippet, micro-post.

**Reading-first**:
The site's design priority. Typography, line-length, whitespace, and code-block legibility take precedence over visual identity, animation, or interactivity. The page gets out of the reader's way.

## Relationships

- A **Post** is independent — no required relationships to other Posts.
- A **TIL** is the short-form sibling of a **Post**; they live in separate feeds and should not share a chronological timeline, so that one doesn't drown out the other.

## Flagged ambiguities

- "Easily maintainable" was initially ambiguous between (a) easy to publish, (b) easy to evolve the design, and (c) long-term resilience. Resolved: priorities are **(c) then (a)**; (b) is explicitly deprioritised.
- "Visually appealing" was initially ambiguous between reading-first, distinctive aesthetic, and interactive/showpiece. Resolved: **reading-first**, modelled on Dan Luu *plus typography care* (constrained max-width, real typeface, minimal header). Not full-Dan-Luu minimalism.
